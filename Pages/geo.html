Geothermal Energy

Sustainabiltiy Renewable

Geothermal energy uses the heat that is naturally produced on Earth to provide both heat and electricity. Through hot water or steam reservoirs, this renewable energy source is able to access the heat that exists at the core of the Earth.

. Geothermal energy is an environmentally friendly substitute for fossil fuels because of its steady and practically endless supply. Geothermal energy uses the heat from the Earth to generate electricity, thereby reducing greenhouse gas emissions and the need for non-renewable resources. Countries like Iceland, the United States, the Philippines, and New Zealand are among the leaders in geothermal energy utilization, demonstrating its global relevance and potential.

Facts about Geo-thermal Energy

Its plentiful supply and low carbon footprint make it sustainable and aid in the fight against climate change. Geothermal power generation is a consistent and dependable energy source that has minimal impact on the environment, unlike fossil fuels, as it is not dependent on limited resources.

Effective geothermal power generation is location-specific, primarily concentrated in regions with high tectonic activity, such as geothermal hotspots near tectonic plate boundaries or volcanic regions.

Geothermal power plants can operate consistently, providing baseload power 24/7, unlike some other renewable energy sources like solar or wind that can be intermittent.
