Tidal Energy

Sustainabiltiy Renewable

Tidal power, a type of renewable energy, generates electricity by harnessing the flow of the ocean's tides. This energy source harnesses the kinetic energy generated by the moon's and sun's gravitational forces, which influence the movement of ocean waters. Tidal power is primarily generated by tidal barrages or tidal stream generators, which use the movement of water to drive turbines and generate electricity.

The consistency and predictability of tidal power ensures its long-term use. Tides are predictable and can be accurately predicted for several years in advance, making them a dependable and renewable source of energy. Furthermore, tidal energy is environmentally friendly, emitting no greenhouse gases during operation and thus contributing to cleaner energy production.

Facts about Nuclear Energy

Coastal regions with strong tidal movements have the potential to generate significant amounts of energy. According to some estimates, tidal energy could meet a significant portion of a country's electricity needs.

Ongoing research and development efforts are aimed at increasing the efficiency and lowering the costs of tidal power generation. Turbine technology advancements aim to improve energy efficiency.
