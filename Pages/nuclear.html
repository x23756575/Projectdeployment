Nuclear Energy

Sustainabiltiy Renewable

Nuclear energy is a sustainable and renewable power source that harnesses controlled nuclear reactions to generate electricity. Through nuclear fission, atomic nuclei are split, releasing large amounts of energy without producing harmful greenhouse gases, thus minimizing its carbon footprint. Nuclear power plants provide a continuous and reliable energy supply while having relatively low costs, making them an efficient and cleaner alternative to fossil fuels.

Nuclear energy has significant advantages, but it also has drawbacks and worries. These include how to handle and store radioactive waste, the high initial building costs, safety concerns, and public perception of the risk of nuclear dangers.
